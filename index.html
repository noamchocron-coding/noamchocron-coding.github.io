RelyOn · 2025–Present · AI Safety

UX Research Program & MVP Definition
for a Consumer Safety App

Designed and facilitated a 10+ session workshop program to evaluate a first-version safety app with real users from scratch — scripting facilitator guides, observing unguided navigation, testing emergency flows and map comprehension. Findings drove the MVP direction and surfaced 5+ UX friction points adopted directly into the product backlog.

User Research Workshop Design MVP Definition Mobile UX Interaction Patterns

The Problem

RelyOn had a working app but no structured feedback on whether real users understood it. The core safety proposition — a "middle layer before calling 911" — needed validation, and the product team had no systematic way to identify which features were confusing and which actually built trust.

What I Did

I wrote a full facilitator script covering: welcome framing, pre-use discussion, unguided first impression, navigation and setup observation, emergency-flow testing, map comprehension, and feature-label interpretation. Sessions were run with participants across age groups and safety contexts — urban commuters, travelers, people living alone.

I also designed an Uber-style issue-lifecycle UX concept: resident submits (text + photos) → manager/vendor resolves with completion note and photo → reporter gets push confirmation. The resolver then chooses visibility: Public (added to a community scrolling feed) or Personal (only the original reporter sees it). This addressed a core HOA resident pain point around "I never know if anything gets done."

Key Findings

  • Feature-clarity gap: "safety companion" and "smart location alert" felt vague to first-time users — who is answering, what do they do?
  • Emergency escalation path unclear: "Is it basically like 9-1-1?" was asked in multiple sessions
  • 3-second SOS countdown: users called it "too slow to open in real danger"
  • Map/pin misinterpretation: users expected to manually drop lightweight alerts, not just view existing ones
  • Retention risk: "I download apps every day and forget about them" — the app hadn't yet earned reflex status

Outcomes

  • 5+ UX improvements prioritized and adopted by the product team
  • Workshop review conclusion: core idea validated; main blocker is trust, not concept
  • MVP recommendation: simple, trust-building emergency flow with first-screen clarity (who answers, when, how it differs from 911 and Life360)
  • Interaction concept (issue-lifecycle flow) used as design reference for HOA dashboard
RelyOn · 2025 · Product Strategy

HOA Market Entry Strategy &
Product Proposition Design

Led end-to-end GTM research across 15+ U.S. regions, identified 50+ target HOAs and property-management firms, and authored a full product proposition for the HOA segment — including MVP scope, competitor gap analysis, pricing benchmarks, and an outreach playbook with 5 tailored templates.

GTM Research Market Segmentation Product Positioning Competitive Analysis Outreach Strategy

The Challenge

RelyOn needed to identify the right U.S. market entry point for a community-safety product with no existing customers. The HOA segment was a hypothesis — not yet validated, not yet positioned, and with no established pricing model or outreach system.

Research Approach

Profiled 15+ U.S. counties by demographics, HOA density, security landscape, income levels, and homeowner pain points. Each region was analyzed for product-market fit and given a tailored positioning angle. Clark County, NV (Las Vegas) was identified as the primary recommended launch market — 3,400+ HOAs, master-planned community clusters, concentrated geography enabling co-development.

Mapped the existing security ecosystem across three models (private security, third-party contractor, community-managed) to identify structural gaps RelyOn could fill. The consistent finding: no player connected residents, HOA boards, and security vendors in real time.

Deliverables

  • 15+ regional profiles with positioning angles and county-level data
  • 50+ target HOAs and property-management firms identified and prioritized
  • Full HOA product proposition with MVP feature scope (alert system, dashboard, incident log)
  • Competitor gap analysis across 4 tiers: security ops, response layer, on-demand responders, consumer awareness
  • HOA SaaS pricing benchmark (10 platforms) informing design-partner offer structure
  • 5 outreach templates + interview script + meeting-prep checklist

Product Concept Developed

RelyOn Connect™: community safety + communication app linking Residents ↔ HOA Board ↔ Security Providers; smart issue reporting, AI alert prioritization, patrol/incident transparency, real-time board dashboard — deployed in ~3 days per community.

Inexto · 2024 · B2B SaaS

Market Requirements Document:
EU Deforestation Regulation Compliance

Authored a full MRD proposing how a B2B SaaS traceability platform could serve EUDR compliance — including market sizing, competitor analysis of 10 providers, user segmentation, feature specs, pricing tiers, and a go-to-market strategy across product, legal, and strategy teams.

MRD Authoring Competitive Analysis Market Sizing B2B SaaS Cross-Functional

Context

The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) requires supply chains for key commodities to be deforestation-free and legally produced, backed by due-diligence statements. Inexto, a supply-chain traceability platform, needed to understand whether and how to build a product for this compliance need.

What I Delivered

A complete Market Requirements Document covering: market definition and regulatory drivers; primary (large multinationals) and secondary (SMEs) segment profiles; market sizing (~50K large EU enterprises + 24M SMEs); competitive analysis of 10 providers including Satelligence, TraceX, KPMG Origins, Osapiens, and Optel; differentiation strategy; pricing model (subscription tiers: ~$1K–$10K/yr); and 5-year revenue projection (~$500K Year 1 → ~$1.5M by Year 5).

Competitive Intelligence

  • Benchmarked 40+ global traceability providers across the broader platform
  • SWOT analyses for Osapiens, Optel, and KPMG Origins
  • Identified key differentiator: comprehensive data integration (satellite + Rainforest Alliance risk tools) + AI analysis + human support — no single competitor combined all three

Cross-Functional Impact

  • Aligned strategy across product, legal, and strategy teams (3 teams)
  • Critiqued 4 internal product pitches on problem framing, visual hierarchy, and value-proposition sequencing
  • Produced Aha! vs. ProductBoard comparative analysis for internal tooling decision
Independent · 2024–2025 · Mobile App

FocusFlow — Local-First
Productivity App (Solo Build)

Designed and built a minimalist, local-first productivity app from scratch — Expo Router, TypeScript, SQLite — shipping modules for goals, habits, finance, fitness, and task history, with a local personalization engine that learns from real usage patterns (no cloud AI, no fake timers).

Mobile Design TypeScript Expo Router SQLite UX Specification

Design Decisions

The core constraint was local-first: no cloud dependency, no internet required, all data on-device. This shaped the entire architecture — a PlannerProvider state layer over SQLite, with seed data for onboarding and a personalization progress bar in Settings showing how the app was learning from the user's actual patterns.

Modules shipped: daily overview, goals/sub-goals, editable plans, task history, habit builder, watchlist, finance tracker, food log, fitness log, and personalization. Each module was specced with detailed implementation prompts for UI refinement — dock design, theme color system, habit-builder integration logic.

What This Demonstrates

  • Full design-to-build fluency: from UX concept to TypeScript implementation
  • Mobile interaction pattern thinking: dock design, navigation hierarchy, module structure
  • Opinionated product decisions: local-first not as a limitation but as a trust feature
  • AI-assisted development workflow: Claude Code + Codex for UI iteration with precise prompting

Stack

  • Expo Router + Expo Go for cross-platform mobile
  • TypeScript throughout
  • SQLite for local persistence
  • PlannerProvider for state management
  • Push notifications for habit reminders

How I Work

01
Start with real users

I run structured workshops before writing specs. Watching someone use a product for the first time — without prompting — is the fastest way to find what the team has stopped seeing. I script facilitator guides, observe unguided navigation, and document confusion as data.

02
Translate, don't transcribe

User feedback and business requirements don't automatically become product decisions. I synthesize: extracting the underlying need behind what someone says, mapping it to what's technically feasible, and framing it in terms the team can act on with a priority attached.

03
Ship a hypothesis, then measure

MVPs work when the team agrees on what they're trying to learn, not just what they're trying to build. I define success metrics upfront — conversion rate, session depth, trust signals — so iteration has direction rather than just momentum.
