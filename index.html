Things I've built

Leona - AI Sales-Assistant Agent

RelyOn's built-in AI sales assistant, helping dealers introduce and sell personal protection clearly and consistently via smart lead discovery, risk intelligence, and a conversion dashboard.

Built on an agentic platform with the Anthropic Claude API and a custom CRM integration. Available through the RelyOn dealer portal, Leona supports outreach, follow-ups, and everyday sales conversations without adding workload or changing how a team operates.

Core capabilities: Smart lead discovery - upload prospect lists and instantly surface high-value conversion opportunities. Risk intelligence engine - advanced AI analyzes neighborhood safety data, crime patterns, and local hazards to identify prospects who need RelyOn's protection most. Conversion dashboard - track the full pipeline from lead to RelyOn user, monitoring conversion rates, safety scores, and engagement trends.

Underlying AI stack: behavioral-analysis AI (payment patterns, service use, tech readiness), pattern-recognition AI that improves with more data, and dynamic message personalization by channel, timing, and content. Segments: Tech Families, Veteran Customers, Young Innovators, Security-Incident Victims.

Implementation: data-prep phase, model adaptation, 10% customer pilot, then full launch. KPIs: conversion rate, time-to-conversion, customer LTV. White-label ready with CRM integration or CSV export.

Also built leona_generator.py, a Python synthetic-data generator producing realistic U.S. demographics by ZIP and state for model training and testing.

FocusFlow - Productivity App

Local-first productivity and life-tracking app with a personalization engine that learns from real usage patterns - no cloud AI, no fake timers.

Built with Expo Router, TypeScript, SQLite, and a local-first storage architecture. State managed via a custom PlannerProvider context. Seed data and notifications are wired in at launch.

Modules include: daily overview, goals and sub-goals with progress tracking, editable plans, task history and graphs, habit builder, watchlist, finance tracker, food journal, fitness module, and a personalization progress bar that reflects real usage over time - not arbitrary onboarding scores.

The personalization engine surfaces patterns from actual user behavior (task completion rates, habit streaks, time-of-day preferences) and adjusts layout priority accordingly. A Settings progress bar shows how personalized the app has become.

Detailed implementation prompts were written for Codex and Claude Code covering UI improvements, history pages, questionnaire flows, dock design, theme color logic, and habit-builder integration.

FOG Trap Prototype - Engineering Design

Led a five-person team to design and build a residential sewer FOG trap at Duke. Iterated from concept through SolidWorks CAD to a working prototype 3D-printed in titanium.

Each day, millions of Americans pour fats, oils, and grease down their sinks. As wastewater flows into trunk lines, FOG clumps form plugs - especially where lines flatten and flow slows. Restaurants must have FOG traps; residences do not, leaving trunk lines vulnerable.

The team's brief: a solution that is durable, cost-efficient, and reproducible. The "clump-stick" concept - protrusions inside the sewer pipe that gather FOG clumps using the density difference between water and FOG - was initially the least favored idea. After rigorous open-minded evaluation, it hit every design criterion. The team iterated through multiple prototypes before delivering a final unit 3D-printed in titanium.
